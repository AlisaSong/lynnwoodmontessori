The Montessori Curriculum

The Montessori educational system, developed by Maria Montessori, is a child-centered approach based on scientific observations of children. Montessori's method has been used for over 100 years in many parts of the world. The Montessori method views the child as the one who is naturally eager for knowledge and capable of initiating learning in a supportive, thoughtfully-repared learning environment.

Learning Outcomes

Children at the Lynnwood Montessori Bilingual Academy are encouraged to nurture and grow their enthusiasm for learning. Our teaching staff cultivates the children's love for work, empathy for others, concentration abilites, a drive for excellence, organizational skills, and a joy of learning. We are inspired when we see our 4 and 5 year olds casually reading to each other, confident in their own ability to grow their reading skills.

Core Curriculum Areas

Every day at our school, we provide children with opportunities to grow their confidence, skills and decision-making abilities across several important areas. At every age level, students learn in different ways at different rates and so they learn much more effectively from direct, hands-on experiences. All lessons are presented individually, through hands-on activities in a sequence from the concrete to the abstract.

Practical Life Areas

Children are offered real life experiences where they learn how to care for themselves and the environment around them. The children come away from these tasks with an understanding of how to accomplish these tasks and the confidence to continue to do so. Activities can include having children pour their own beverages, water the plants in the classroom, and keeping our shared space organized. Children also learn the healthy manners of taking turns, waiting to do the "work" they desire, taking constructive feedback in a positive way, and being thoughtful of another child's feelings and needs.

Sensorial Activities

In this area of learning, we use the children's senses to help them become more logical, aware and perceptive. Sensorial activies can include working with visual shapes, using different instruments to work with sound, a tasting tray to experience different tastes, bottles for smelling, and mystery bags for tactile exercises.

Language Arts and Written Communication

These activities are organized in a sequential manner to follow the natural development of language for the child. Throughout the day, we read books together with the children, share stories, and sing songs. The children also learn to listen to others speak during circle time.

We intentionally phonetically teach the sounds of the alphabet in a natural way for the children, so that they can start forming letters and words and begin to sound out short words. Vocabulary development is emphasized in all areas by using specific words for objects in the classroom.

At approximate 4 1/2 years, the children begin to put phonetic sounds together to read short words, and by 5 years old, the children can express their love for reading and writing.

Music Curriculum

We teach the fundamentals of music through vocal, instrumental, movement, and listening skills. The curriculum consists of singing, playing instruments, listening to music, music theory, creative movement, music history, and multi-cultural music experiences with Montessori Bells.

Movement and Yoga

The Montessori movement program is fun and effective exercise designed for children. It develops and refines individual agility, balance and coordination skills as well as focusing on motor skills and spatial awareness. Throughout the class, all the children are participating in fun movements. A veriety of equipment is used throughout the sessions including hoops, beanbags, juggling scarves, and rhythym sticks, allowing each child to practice their concentration, balancing and listening skills. Overall the class aims to encourage all children to enjoy and replicate fun non-competitive games and help them discover the joys of a healthy, active lifestyle.
